Last updated: June 16, 2026

Jacob Rozell ("we", "us") built AshVault as a free offline game. This policy explains what information the app handles.

Summary

  • On your device: display name, run progress, prestige, mercenaries, relics, and settings.
  • Sent from App Store (Release) builds only: limited anonymous usage analytics and crash reports via Google Firebase.
  • Not collected: accounts, ads, precise location, contacts, or photos.

Information stored on your device

The app saves gameplay data using iOS local storage (UserDefaults), including:

  • A display name you enter at the start of a run
  • In-progress run state, prestige progress, mercenaries, relics, and settings
  • Audio and automation preferences

This data stays on your device unless you delete the app or reset your device.

Information sent to third parties

In Release builds installed from the App Store (not typical Debug or developer builds), the app may send limited diagnostic and usage data to Google Firebase:

Firebase Analytics

We collect a small, allowlisted set of anonymous product-health events, such as:

  • app_open — app finished launching
  • run_started, run_ended (defeat or abandon)
  • layer_cleared, dragon_slayed, prestige_completed
  • onboarding_completed

Parameters are restricted to non-identifying technical context (layer, level, shard counts, app version). We do not send player names or combat log content.

Firebase Crashlytics

If the app crashes, Crashlytics may receive crash stack traces, app version, and OS version needed to diagnose the problem.

When Firebase is disabled

  • Debug builds from Xcode
  • Builds without a valid production Firebase configuration
  • Automated tests (CI uses a placeholder plist)

Google's privacy practices: firebase.google.com/support/privacy

What we do not collect

  • No advertising or ad-network SDKs
  • No cross-app tracking for ads
  • No sale of personal information
  • No in-app purchases

Third-party links

Settings may link to external sites (for example, Buy Me a Coffee). Those sites have their own privacy policies.

Children's privacy

AshVault is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone.
